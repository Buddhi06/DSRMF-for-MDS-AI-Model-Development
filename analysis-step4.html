Phase 1: Step 1:
Step 1.3 - Estimation of risk(s) for each applicable threat and vulnerability combination

Security risk is estimated by combining the likelihood that a threat will successfully exploit a vulnerability with the severity of the resulting impact.

Conducting risk estimation includes the following tasks:

  • Task 1: Determine Likelihood of occcurence (non-adversarial threats)/initiation (adversarial threats)
  • Task 2: Determine Impact
  • Task 3: Determine Risk

Task 1: Determine Likelihood of Occurrence/Initiation

Likelihood of occurrence is a weighted factor based on a subjective analysis of the probability that a given threat is capable of exploiting a given vulnerability (or set of vulnerabilities).

The following likelihood factors need to be considered to assess the likelihood of occurrence of adversarial threats:

  • Adversary intent - The motivation, goals, and willingness of an attacker to carry out the threat event
  • Adversary capability - The attacker's technical skills, resources and overall ability to successfully carry out the threat
  • Adversary targeting - The targeted asset

  • For non-adversarial threat events, the likelihood of occurrence is estimated by: • Historical evidence or empirical data on the threat

During calculation, a likelihood level is assigned to each likelihood factor,and an overall likelihood level is then calculated by taking the average of all the likelihood factors.

Following assessment scales can be used for assessing the likelihood of threat event initiation or occurrence:

Task 2: Determine Impact

Impact is defined as the extent to which a threat event might affect the MDS AI model. When assessing impact, following impact factors need to be taken into consideration:

  1. Harm to operations
  2. Harm to assets
  3. Harm to individuals
  4. Reputational harm
  5. Financial loss

During the calculation, an impact level is assigned to each impact factor, and an overall impact level is then calculated by taking the average of all the impact factors.

Following assessment scales can be used for assessing the impact of threat events:

Impact of Threat Events
Assessment Scale - Impact of Threat Events
Qualitative Values Semi-Quantitative Values Description
Very High 96-100 10 The threat event could be expected to have multiple severe or catastrophic adverse effects on operations, assets, individuals, reputational harm or financial loss.
High 80-95 8 The threat event could be expected to have severe or catastrophic adverse effects on operations, assets, individuals, reputational harm or financial loss.
Moderate 21-79 5 The threat event could be expected to have serious adverse effects on operations, assets, individuals, reputational harm or financial loss.
Low 5-20 2 The threat event could be expected to have limited adverse effects on operations, assets, individuals, reputational harm or financial loss.
Very Low 0-4 0 The threat event could be expected to have negligible adverse effects on operations, assets, individuals, reputational harm or financial loss.

Task 3: Determine Risk (Semi-Quantitative and Qualitative)

(1) Semi-Quantitative - Risk Score Calculation

Once the overall likelihood and overall impact have been determined, the level of risk is calculated by combining the two.

The risk score can be calculated using the formula below:

Risk Score = Overall Likelihood × Overall Impact
