Identification of adverse impact

Adverse impact of a data security breach in AI models can be described in terms of loss or degradation of confidentiality, integrity, availability, authenticity, authorisation and non-repudiation of data. AAMI TIR 57 provides a set of questions that should be answered when identifying the adverse impact of compromised assets. This framework has extended the set of questions by adding the three additional security properties, i.e., authenticity, authorisation and non-repudiation. At least the following questions should be answered to identify the impact of asset compromise:

  1. What is the impact if that asset’s confidentiality was compromised, and the information it contained was available to an attacker?
  2. What is the impact if that asset’s integrity was compromised?
  3. What is the impact if that asset could be made unavailable?
  4. What is the impact if that asset’s authenticity was compromised?
  5. What is the impact if that asset’s authorisation was compromised?
  6. What is the impact if that asset’s non-repudiation was compromised?
  7. Could the immediate impact of an asset compromise lead to another type of attack or vulnerability?

The developer team have to review each threat and vulnreabilty and ask the above questions to identify the adverse impacts.
For example: If the attacker initiates a DoS attack on the ML platform and makes the service unavailable, it will have an impact of the operation of the AI model and acheiveing the intended service of the MDS AI model.
The team should document the adverse impact of each threat and vulnerabilty in the risk assessment report.
