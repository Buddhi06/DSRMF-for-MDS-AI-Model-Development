Task 03: - Identify assumptions and constraints

    Next

Organisation should identify assumptions in key areas relevant to the risk assessment including the following:

Threats – Identify which threats are to be considered during risk assessments and make explicit the process used to identify threats and any assumptions related to the threat identification process [2]. “The framework proposes to use the STRIDE threat modelling to identify the threats and use the list of threats provided in the framework as a starting point.”

Vulnerabilities – Identify which types of vulnerabilities that are to be considered during risk assessment [2]. Moreover, the explicit process used to identify vulnerabilities and any assumptions related to the vulnerability identification process should be identified [2]. “The framework proposes to use the STRIDE threat modelling to identify the vulnerabilities and use the list of vulnerabilities provided in the framework as a starting point.”

Likelihood – Organisation make explicit the process used to conduct the likelihood determinations and any assumptions related to the likelihood determination process [2]. “The framework proposes to use the likelihood scales provided in the Determine Likelihood of occurrence step of the framework.

Impacts – Organisation determines potential adverse impacts and any assumptions related to the impact determination process [2]. “The framework proposes to assess the impacts in terms of loss or degradation of confidentiality, integrity, availability, authenticity, authorisation and non-repudiation of data as presented in adverse impact determination step of the risk assessment process.”

Risk Tolerance and Uncertainty – Organisation determines the levels of risks that are acceptable [2]. “The framework proposes to consider the risk that are very low and low as acceptable, moderate as tolerable and high and very high as unacceptable as presented in the Risk Evaluation step.”

Analytical approach – Selecting the analytical approach includes selecting both assessment approach and analysis approach [2]. “The framework proposes to use qualitative and/or semi-quantitative approaches as the assessment approach and asset/impact-oriented approach as the analysis approach.”
