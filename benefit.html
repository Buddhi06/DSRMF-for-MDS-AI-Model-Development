Phase 2: Step 4:
Benefit Risk Analysis

A benefit-risk analysis should be performed for the residual risks that are judged unacceptable (identified in setp 3) and for which further risk control is not practicable. The benefit-risk analysis is used to determine whether the residual risk is outweighed by the expected benefits of the intended use of the MDS AI model. It is important to note that practicability of further risk reduction should be evaluated before considering a benefit-risk analysis.The AI model should not be deployed if the benefits do not outweigh the risks or until additional risk reduction measures are applied.

For performing benefit-risk analysis, the following steps need to be followed:

  1. Benefit calculation
  2. Risk calculation
  3. Benefit-Risk ratio calculation
  4. Determine acceptability

(1) Benefit Calculation

The following factors need to be considered during benefit identification:

  1. The clinical performance expected from the use of MDS AI model during clinical use.
  2. The clinical benefit expected from that performance.

The clinical performance and clinical benefit can be differentiated as follows:

Category Description
Clinical Performance The ability of the MDS AI model to achieve its intended medical purpose, thereby leading to a clinical benefit.
Clinical Benefit The positive impact of using the MDS AI model on an individual’s health, expressed in terms of meaningful, measurable, patient-relevant clinical outcome(s) such as improvements in the quality of life, symptom relief, pain relief, enhanced diagnosis etc...

The following aspects should be taken into account when calculating the benefit:

  • Type of expected clinical benefits for the patient - For example, whether the AI model performs a significant role in a life-saving medical device or is essential in a specific medical scenario.
  • Probability that the patient will experience the expected clinical benefits - The likelihood that the MDS AI model is effective in performing the clinical function.
  • Magnitude of the expected clinical benefits - The degree to which the patient will experience the clinical benefit.

Probability of benefit experience

The following assessment scales can be used to assess the probability that the patient will experience the expected clinical benefit:

Assessment Scales - Probability of clinical benefit experience
Qualitative Values Semi-Quantitative Values Description
Very High 96–100 10 It almost certain to experience the expected clinical benefit
High 80–95 8 It is highly likely to experience the expected clinical benefit.
Moderate 21–79 5 It is somewhat likely to experience the expected clinical benefit.
Low 5–20 2 It is unlikely to experience the expected clinical benefit.
Very Low 0–4 0 It is highly unlikely to experience the expected clinical benefit.

A probability value need to be assigned to the identified benefit based on the above assessment scales.

Magnitude of the expected clinical benefits

The following assessment scales can be used to assess the magnitude of the expected clinical benefits:

Assessment Scales - Magnitude of the expected clinical benefits
Qualitative Values Semi-Quantitative Values Description
Very High 96–100 10 The expected clinical benefit is critical or transformative, with a major positive impact on patient outcomes (e.g., life-saving, major improvement in quality of life).
High 80–95 8 The expected clinical benefit is significant, providing clear clinical improvements (e.g., improved diagnosis accuracy, faster treatment, reduced complications).
Moderate 21–79 5 The expected clinical benefit is moderate, offering measurable clinical value (e.g., enhanced monitoring, reduced clinician workload).
Low 5–20 2 The expected clinical benefit is limited, with minor improvements to existing clinical processes or outcomes.
Very Low 0–4 0 The expected clinical benefit is minimal or negligible, with little to no meaningful impact on clinical outcomes.

A magnitude value need to be assigned to the identified benefit based on the above assessment scales.

After identifying the probability and magnitude, the benefit value can be calculated using the following equation:

Benefit Value = Probability * Magnitude

Probability – The probability that the patient will experience the expected clinical benefit
Magnitude – The magnitude of the expected clinical benefit

(2) Risk Calculation

The risk values are already calculated in the Residual Risk Evaluation phase.The values for each unacceptable residual risks, for which further risk control is not possible, are considered during this step.

(3) Benefit-risk Ratio Calculation

Once the benefit value and risk values are calculated, the benefit-risk ratio can be determined using the formula below:

Benefit-Risk Ratio = Benefit Value/Risk Value

📊 Benefit-risk Ratio Calculator

It should be noted that this approach assigns a value to each individual benefit and risk; it does not sum all risks or benefits to obtain a single value for each. This results in the calculation of a pairwise comparison between each risk and each benefit.

(4) Determine Acceptability

Any benefit-risk ratio greater than 1 is considered favorable, as the benefits outweigh the risks (i.e., the benefit value is greater than the risk value).
